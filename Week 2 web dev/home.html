Pistols: A handgun designed to be fired with one hand Often used for self-defense, law enforcement, and military purposes Can be semi-automatic (fires one round per trigger pull) or automatic (fires multiple rounds per trigger pull) Submachine guns: A type of firearm that is smaller and lighter than a rifle but fires pistol-caliber ammunition Can be fully automatic or select-fire (allows the shooter to switch between semi-automatic and automatic modes) Used primarily in military and law enforcement applications Shotguns: A type of firearm that is designed to fire a group of small pellets (shot) or a single solid projectile (slug) Used for hunting, sport shooting, and self-defense Can have either a smoothbore (no rifling in the barrel) or a rifled barrel (spiral grooves cut into the barrel to impart spin to the projectile) Can also have various types of actions (the method by which the gun is loaded, fired, and unloaded), including pump-action, semi-automatic, and break-action.
